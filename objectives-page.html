OBJECTIVES
The Association of Computer Studies Students (ACSS) Organization aims to provide ideas and information
about the computer-related courses, subjects and possible career paths. This is aided with the development of skills and talents, not just academically but excel as well in extra-curricular.

The ACSS aim to organize various events such as seminars, quiz bees, e-sports competitions and outreach,
serve as a bridge between the members and the rest of the university, create connections with ICT professionals, and foster camaraderie within the organization. The ACSS Organization also aims to help students leverage their being tech-savvy and make a difference. The ACSS are totally an organization about a fairly modern yet socially relevant field of study that you definitely don't wanna miss.
GOALS
As a self-governing recognized student organization,
the ACSS envision themselves into being the center of excellence in a fast-growing community while continuously upholding the commitment to developing the students' skills, interests, and well-being.

The ACSS also aims to create opportunities for students to be actively involved and offer their contributions while socializing and having fun.

ADVOCACY
The Association of Computer Studies and Systems Students (ACSS) strongly supports the Central Student Council
to push the refund of unused school fees and freeze or reasonably lessen the 5% surcharge placed on late tuition payments,
because we believe that the university should give its utmost consideration on the call and appeal of the students

We are firm to our stand that the underused school facilities, benefits and services not utilized with the remaining month of
the academic year should be refunded. In addition, we are resolutely condemning the unjustifiable 5% surcharge placed on late tuition payment,
in view of the fact that the University of the East commits itself to produce quality and affordable education, and the surcharge placed will
just add more burden to students who are already struggling to pay the huge amount of tuition fee imposed by the University.

As an organization, we believe that the University of the East should consider the welfare and safety of the students and their families
due to the pandemic COVID-19, that impedes the financial stability of some indigent families. The University of the East,
as an institution of higher learning should be more considerate and make adjustments for the betterment of the students,
because education shouldn't be limited to the wealthiest few.

We are encouraging everyone to express their utmost support to Central Student Council in their fight for our rights.
